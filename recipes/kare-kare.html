Recipe for Kare-Kare

Description

Kare-kare is a stew made with oxtail, tripe, and vegetables, such as eggplant, string beans, and pechay, in a thick peanut sauce. It is often served with bagoong, a fermented shrimp paste, and rice.

The dish is known for its thick, savory sauce made from ground roasted peanuts and rice flour, which gives it a nutty flavor and a slightly sweet taste.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. oxtail, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 lb. tripe, cleaned and cut into small pieces
  • 2-3 cups water
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil
  • 1 cup ground roasted peanuts
  • 2 tbsp. rice flour
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp. atsuete powder (annatto powder)
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch of pechay (bok choy), chopped
  • 1 cup string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 banana blossom, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Bagoong (shrimp paste) for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring the oxtail and tripe to a boil with 2-3 cups of water. Reduce heat and let simmer for 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender. Add more water as needed.
  2. In a separate pan, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and saut√© until fragrant.
  3. Add the ground peanuts and rice flour to the pan and stir until well combined. Cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the mixture turns brown.
  4. Add the achuete powder to the pan and stir to distribute the color evenly.
  5. Pour the peanut mixture into the pot with the oxtail and tripe. Add more water if needed to achieve the desired consistency.
  6. Add the eggplant, pechay, string beans, and banana blossom to the pot. Let simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the vegetables are cooked through.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot with bagoong and freshly cooked rice.

Note: You can adjust the amount of vegetables to your liking, and you can also use other meats or seafood instead of oxtail and tripe.
Some recipes also add peanut butter or toasted rice instead of ground peanuts and rice flour.
