Recipe for Adobo

Description

Adobo is a popular dish in Filipino cuisine that is made with meat (usually chicken or pork) that is marinated and cooked in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and other spices.

The dish has a savory and slightly tangy flavor and is often served with rice. Adobo is considered the national dish of the Philippines and is a staple in many Filipino households.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. chicken or pork, cut into serving pieces
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp. whole peppercorns
  • 2-3 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tbsp. cooking oil

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, marinate the chicken or pork in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and whole peppercorns for at least 30 minutes or up to overnight.
  2. In a large pot or pan, heat the cooking oil over medium-high heat. Add the marinated meat and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Add the marinade, dried bay leaves, and water to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let simmer for 30-45 minutes, or until the meat is cooked through and tender.
  4. Serve hot with rice.

Note: You can adjust the amount of vinegar and soy sauce to your liking. Some recipes also add sugar or coconut milk to the adobo for added sweetness or creaminess.
