Recipe for Chicharon Bulaklak

Description

Chicharon bulaklak is a popular Filipino snack made from deep-fried pork intestines. The dish gets its name from the appearance of the crispy fried intestines, which resemble flowers or "bulaklak" in Tagalog.

Chicharon bulaklak is often served as a crunchy and savory appetizer or as a beer pairing during social gatherings or street food markets in the Philippines.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork intestines, thoroughly cleaned and cut into bite-size pieces
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Cooking oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, boil the pork intestines in water until tender. This may take around 1 to 2 hours. Drain the intestines and set them aside.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix together the minced garlic, salt, vinegar, and water to make the seasoning.
  3. Heat up enough oil in a deep fryer or a large wok to deep fry the pork intestines.
  4. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the pork intestines to the oil and deep-fry until crispy and golden brown. This may take around 5 to 7 minutes.
  5. Once the pork intestines are crispy and golden brown, remove them from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain them on a paper towel-lined plate to remove any excess oil.
  6. While the chicharon bulaklak is still hot, pour the seasoning over the top and mix well to ensure that each piece is coated evenly.
  7. Serve with cold beer and vinegar sauce. Enjoy!

Note: Be careful when working with hot oil, and make sure to use caution to avoid any burns or injuries.
