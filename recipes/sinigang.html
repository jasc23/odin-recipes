Recipe for Sinigang

Description

Sinigang is a sour soup made with tamarind as the souring agent. It is usually made with pork, beef, or fish, and vegetables like kangkong, radish, and tomatoes. Other souring agents like guava, calamansi, or kamias can also be used to make the soup.

The sourness of the broth can be adjusted by adding more or less tamarind, and the dish is often served with rice. Sinigang is a staple in many Filipino households and is enjoyed all year round, but it is especially popular during the colder months.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. pork belly, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 10 cups water
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1-2 pieces radish, sliced
  • 1-2 pieces eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach), chopped
  • 1 cup tamarind pulp
  • Fish sauce or salt to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring the pork and water to a boil. Reduce heat and let simmer for 1-2 hours, or until the meat is tender.
  2. Skim off any impurities that rise to the surface of the broth.
  3. Add the onion, garlic, and tomatoes to the pot and let simmer for another 10-15 minutes, or until the vegetables are soft.
  4. Add the radish and eggplant to the pot and let simmer for another 5-10 minutes.
  5. In a separate bowl, dissolve the tamarind pulp in 2 cups of water. Strain the mixture into the pot, discarding any solids.
  6. Add the kangkong to the pot and let simmer for 1-2 minutes, or until the leaves are wilted.
  7. Season with fish sauce or salt to taste.
  8. Serve hot with freshly cooked rice.

Note: You can adjust the amount of tamarind pulp to your liking, and you can also use other meats or seafood instead of pork.
Some recipes also add other souring agents, such as kamias or calamansi, instead of tamarind.
